# Demo Video Script — Next Move Planner

Target length: 3:30–4:30. Format: screen recording with voiceover; presenter cam optional for open/close. Tone: calm, specific, zero hype — the same voice as the product.

---

## 1. Problem (0:00–0:40)

*[Screen: a property listing site, then a bank borrowing calculator]*

> Every year, hundreds of thousands of Australian homeowners face the same question: can we buy our next home before we sell this one?
>
> And here's the strange thing — there's nowhere to get an honest answer. Bank calculators tell you how much you can borrow. Property portals tell you what your home might be worth. But nobody answers the actual question: am I ready, and in what order should I do this?
>
> So people guess. They delay the broker conversation because they don't feel ready. They buy under pressure, sell under more pressure, and around $8,000 later, they've moved twice.

## 2. Customer need (0:40–1:10)

*[Screen: persona snapshots from the research docs]*

> I researched five types of movers — upsizers, downsizers, growing families, empty nesters, and self-employed homeowners. Different lives, same pattern: the broker conversation is gated by a private judgement — "are we viable?" — that people make alone, with bad tools.
>
> The insight: readiness, not rates, is the bottleneck. Whoever helps a homeowner feel genuinely ready earns the right to the next conversation.

## 3. Product vision (1:10–1:40)

*[Screen: planner landing page]*

> This is the Next Move Planner — a concept I built for Bridgit, whose whole brand is moving forward with confidence. The idea: move that confidence upstream. Before the application. Before the broker. Five minutes, six questions, no sign-up, nothing stored.

## 4. Prototype walkthrough (1:40–3:10)

*[Screen: live walkthrough with the Upsizer's numbers — $1.2M home, $450k owing, $1.65M target, $240k income, 6 months]*

> Six questions, one at a time, in plain language. Notice what it doesn't ask — no credit history, no payslips.
>
> *[Results render]*
>
> The readiness score is built the way a bridging lender actually thinks: peak borrowing across both properties, what you'd carry after selling, and how hard your income works. Deterministic, auditable maths — the AI explains the result in plain language, but it never decides it. In consumer lending, that separation matters.
>
> *[Scroll to strategies]*
>
> Three ways to sequence the move — buy first, sell first, or wait — each with honest trade-offs. And yes, this tool will tell you to wait. A readiness tool that always says yes is just a lead form.
>
> *[Scenario modeller]*
>
> The biggest fear in buying first is "what if our place doesn't sell?" So we answer it in dollars: a 30, 60, or 90-day sale, the carrying cost of each, and exactly what you'd do in that scenario.
>
> *[Next steps + broker questions]*
>
> It ends where it should: a concrete plan, and five questions to ask a broker — turning a sales conversation into a planning one.

## 5. AI opportunities (3:10–3:40)

*[Screen: ai-opportunities.md priority table]*

> The planner is one slice of a bigger thesis. Across Bridgit's journey, the highest-leverage AI wins are language and document tasks, not credit decisioning: document intelligence at application, status narration through settlement, and this explanation layer at evaluation. Decisioning stays deterministic until model-risk governance earns more.

## 6. Why this matters for Bridgit (3:40–4:20)

*[Screen: roadmap / white-label slide, then presenter]*

> Bridgit has accredited most of Australia's broker network and white-labels with the major aggregators. That's the real prize here: a planner brokers embed with their own clients turns readiness into distribution.
>
> Every assessment completed is a homeowner who understands bridging before they apply — better-qualified, better-educated, arriving with realistic expectations. That's cheaper acquisition, higher approval rates, and a brand that owns the start of the journey, not just the loan in the middle.
>
> This is how I'd work as a senior PM at Bridgit: find the question nobody's answering, validate it with discovery, prototype the answer, and ship the smallest thing that proves it. Thanks for watching.
