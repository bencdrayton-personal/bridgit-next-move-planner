# Journey Maps — Current State & Future State

## Current state: the property transition today

Mapped for the Upsizer persona (the highest-volume segment); divergences for other personas noted inline.

| Stage | Customer doing | Customer feeling | Pain points | Today's touchpoints |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** ("we've outgrown this place") | Browsing listings casually; saving properties; talking at dinner | Hopeful, vague | No idea what's affordable; "someday" has no shape | REA/Domain, social media, friends |
| **Research** ("could we actually?") | Online valuations of own home; bank borrowing calculators; reading articles | Curious → quietly anxious | Calculators answer loan size, not sequencing; conflicting numbers; bridging finance invisible or scary-sounding | Bank calculators, portals, forums |
| **Finance** ("we should talk to someone") | Delaying the broker call; gathering documents reluctantly | Intimidated, unprepared | Doesn't know what to ask; fears being judged or "locked in"; self-employed segment expects rejection | Broker (eventually), lender sites |
| **Purchase** ("we found it") | Inspecting, bidding, panicking about timing | Excited + terrified | Pre-approval may not exist yet; pressure to make unconditional offers; fear of buying before selling without understanding bridging | Agents, auctions, broker (rushed) |
| **Sale** ("now sell ours — fast") | Listing under time pressure; accepting compromise offers | Pressured, regretful | Sale becomes forced by purchase settlement; presentation rushed; price suffers | Selling agent |
| **Settlement** ("just make it work") | Juggling dates, removalists, possibly rent/storage gap | Exhausted | Double moves; bridging discovered too late to use well; $8k+ in avoidable costs | Conveyancers, lenders, removalists |

**The structural failure:** finance understanding arrives at the *Purchase* stage, when it was needed at *Research*. Every downstream pain compounds from that one mis-sequencing.

**Persona divergences:**
- *Downsizer:* the Sale stage is the emotional centre (decades of belongings, open homes feel invasive); Research can last years.
- *Growing Family:* Finance is the wall — thin equity means the journey often ends there, without a clear "what would make this possible".
- *Self-Employed:* Finance is avoided entirely based on past bank trauma; they skip to Purchase and improvise.

## Future state: with the Next Move Planner

The intervention point is the Research stage. One new step changes the shape of everything after it.

| Stage | New experience | AI's role | Outcome shift |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | Planner discoverable from content ("can I buy before I sell?" searches), portals, broker sites | — | Bridging enters the consideration set early, framed as "sequencing choice", not "exotic loan" |
| **Research** | **5-minute readiness assessment** → score, equity position, strategy comparison, scenario modelling | Plain-language narrative of position; jargon-free explanation of trade-offs | The private "are we viable?" question gets an honest, structured answer months earlier |
| **Finance** | Arrives at broker with a plan: known position, prepared questions, calibrated expectations | Generated broker-question list; preparation checklist | Broker conversation starts at "how", not "whether"; higher-quality applications; fewer dead-end consults |
| **Purchase** | Buys knowing the bridge maths: peak position, carrying cost per month of sale time | Scenario modelling stress-tests the timeline before the offer | Confident unconditional offers where justified; no panic-bridging |
| **Sale** | Sells on own timeline from a settled position; sale prep started in parallel (planner's checklist) | Re-run plan as real numbers replace estimates | Better presentation, better price, shorter bridge |
| **Settlement** | One move. Bridge closes as planned | Post-settlement check-in: "your plan vs what happened" (H2) | The planner's promise — *move once, with confidence* — kept measurably |

**Design principle for the future state:** the planner never replaces the broker — it *upgrades the customer who walks into the broker's office*. This keeps the product aligned with Bridgit's channel strategy (broker network as the distribution asset) rather than in tension with it.

## Moments that matter (prioritised)

1. **The first honest verdict** (Research) — the moment of differentiation; must feel like clarity, not a sales gate
2. **The 90-day scenario** (Research) — directly defuses the #1 fear ("what if it doesn't sell?") with dollars and actions
3. **Walking into the broker prepared** (Finance) — where planner value becomes pipeline value; measured by referral conversion
4. **The "not yet" path** (Research) — turning a no into a plan with a review date; the longest-horizon trust builder
