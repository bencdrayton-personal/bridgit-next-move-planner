# Product Vision — Next Move Planner

## The one-line vision

Every Australian homeowner should know whether they can buy before they sell — before they talk to anyone.

## Why this, why Bridgit

Bridgit's positioning is not fundamentally about lending. It is about reducing uncertainty during property transitions: *move forward with confidence*. But today, Bridgit's product surface begins at the application. The confidence problem begins months earlier.

A homeowner's transition has a long, anxious pre-application phase: "Can we actually do this? What's our equity really worth? What happens if our house takes three months to sell?" Today those questions get answered by guesswork, generic calculators, or a broker conversation the customer doesn't yet feel ready to have. Most people who could use a bridging solution never discover they're eligible for one — bridging finance remains one of the least understood products in Australian consumer lending despite solving one of the most common problems (the buy-sell sequencing gap).

The Next Move Planner moves Bridgit's value proposition upstream: from "fast approval when you apply" to "clarity before you commit to anything."

## Vision statement

> Within three years, the Next Move Planner is the way Australians start a property transition — a free, trusted readiness check that turns "I have no idea if we can do this" into "I know my position, my options, and my next step." For Bridgit, it is the top of the funnel: the most qualified, best-educated borrowers in the market arriving at application already understanding the product.

## What this is not

- Not a loan application or pre-qualification tool — no credit checks, no stored personal data, no credit decisions
- Not a property valuation product — it takes customer estimates and teaches healthy scepticism about them
- Not financial advice — it is structured education that makes the broker conversation better, not a substitute for it

## Strategic bet

**The bet:** homeowners who understand their position before applying convert at materially higher rates, with lower acquisition cost and fewer abandoned applications, than homeowners acquired at the application step.

**Why we believe it:** bridging is a considered, high-stakes, low-frequency decision. Confidence is the bottleneck, not interest rates. The lender that builds confidence earliest in the journey gets first call when the customer is ready — and earns broker goodwill by sending through educated, qualified referrals.

## Three horizons

| Horizon | Product | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| H1 (0–6 mo) | Readiness assessment, strategy comparison, scenario modelling — anonymous, consumer-facing | Prove engagement and broker-referral intent |
| H2 (6–18 mo) | Personalised AI guidance, saved plans, valuation data integration, broker handoff with consent | Prove conversion lift into qualified applications |
| H3 (18 mo+) | White-label planner for aggregator partners; readiness signals feeding faster approvals | Planner becomes distribution — embedded where brokers already work |

The H3 white-label angle matters: Bridgit has accredited over three quarters of Australia's broker network and runs white-label solutions with Connective, Aussie, Finsure and Loan Market. A planner that brokers embed in their own client conversations multiplies distribution rather than competing with it.

## Success, in one sentence

A homeowner finishes the planner and says: *"I finally understand our position"* — and when they're ready, Bridgit is the obvious next call.
